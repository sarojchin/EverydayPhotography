## Lesson 1 — Selfies: Lighting and Angles
4 slides · 5 min

### Slide 1 — Selfies: Lighting and Angles [Teach]
Great selfies come down to two things: where the light comes from, and where you hold your camera. This lesson covers both.

### Slide 2 — Front Window Light [Teach]
Stand facing a window so the light hits your face directly. Hold your phone slightly above eye level and look toward the camera. This works because the light covers your whole face evenly with no strong shadows, making skin look smoother and eyes brighter. Everything appears clear and easy to see.

### Slide 3 — Side Window Light [Teach]
Stand so the window is on your left or right side. Turn your face slightly toward the light so both sides are still visible. The light hits one side more than the other, making features stand out more—jawline and cheekbones become more noticeable.

### Slide 4 — Pose [Teach]
Keep lighting the same, then adjust how you're standing. Don't face straight on—turn slightly. Push chin forward a little, then lower it slightly. Take several photos with small changes. The order matters: get the light right first, then adjust your angle, then your pose.

## Lesson 2 — Control the Frame
4 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — The Frame Is Everything [Teach]
The camera sees everything. You decide what matters. A strong photo has one clear subject and no distractions.

### Slide 2 — Clean vs Messy Frame [Teach]
Take a random photo. Then improve it by moving your position, adjusting your angle, or removing distractions.

### Slide 3 — Tight vs Loose Framing [Teach]
Tight framing: your subject fills the frame. Loose framing: more of the environment shows.

### Slide 4 — What Changes [Teach]
Small position changes dramatically change quality.

### Slide 5 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 5 photos of the same subject with different backgrounds. Keep only the best one.

**Shots:**
- Move your position
- Adjust your angle
- Remove one distraction
- Try tighter framing
- Try looser framing

## Lesson 3 — Angles Change Your Face
4 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Same Face, Different Result [Teach]
Angle changes proportions instantly.

### Slide 2 — Eye Level [Teach]
A neutral, straight-on view. Honest but flat.

### Slide 3 — Above Eye Level [Teach]
Camera above eye level is more flattering: larger eyes, cleaner jawline.

### Slide 4 — Below Eye Level [Teach]
Camera below eye level creates a stronger presence but is less flattering.

### Slide 5 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 3 selfies from different angles and choose the best.

**Shots:**
- Eye level — straight on
- Above eye level — camera raised
- Below eye level — camera lowered

## Lesson 4 — Natural Posing
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Stop Posing [Teach]
Good photos look unplanned.

### Slide 2 — Micro Adjustments [Teach]
Small changes make a big difference: head tilt, chin forward or down, shoulder angle, eye direction.

### Slide 3 — Expression Layer [Teach]
Try different expressions: full smile, neutral, mid-laugh, looking away.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 10 selfies with different micro-adjustments. Pick the 2 most natural.

**Shots:**
- Head tilt left
- Head tilt right
- Chin forward + down
- Shoulder angled
- Eyes looking away
- Full smile
- Neutral
- Mid-laugh
- Relaxed and off-guard
- Your best natural

## Lesson 5 — Sharpness + Distance
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Blur Comes From You [Teach]
Not the camera. Movement and distance matter most.

### Slide 2 — Stability Test [Teach]
Try holding your phone different ways: one hand, two hands, elbows tucked in, resting on a surface.

### Slide 3 — Distance Test [Teach]
Close distance distorts. Mid distance looks natural. Far distance looks balanced.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 6 photos testing stability and distance. Select the sharpest.

**Shots:**
- One hand
- Two hands
- Elbows tucked
- Phone resting on surface
- Close distance
- Mid distance

## Lesson 6 — Group Selfies
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Group Structure [Teach]
Group photos fail when people are unevenly arranged.

### Slide 2 — Positioning [Teach]
Place taller people slightly back. Keep faces at different levels. Avoid straight-line rows.

### Slide 3 — Focus Control [Teach]
Make sure everyone is visible and not blocked by anyone else.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 3 group selfies with different arrangements and choose the clearest one.

**Shots:**
- Straight-line row
- Staggered heights
- Different face levels

## Lesson 7 — Photographing Other People
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Don't Control Too Much [Teach]
People should look natural, not staged.

### Slide 2 — Give Simple Direction [Teach]
Keep direction minimal: turn slightly, relax shoulders, change the angle gently.

### Slide 3 — Capture Moments [Teach]
Take multiple shots, not single attempts. The best frame usually isn't the first.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 10 photos of another person and select the best candid-looking one.

**Shots:**
- Ask them to turn slightly
- Ask them to relax their shoulders
- Change your angle
- Zoom in closer
- Step back wider
- Catch them mid-movement
- Catch them mid-laugh
- Try a second location
- Wait for a quiet moment
- Let them forget the camera

## Lesson 8 — Harsh Outdoor Light
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Sunlight Is Unforgiving [Teach]
Direct sun creates strong shadows.

### Slide 2 — Turn, Don't Fight [Teach]
Turn your face slightly away from direct sun. Use partial shade instead of standing in full shade or full sun.

### Slide 3 — Control Shadows [Teach]
Use angles to reduce harsh contrast across your face.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 5 outdoor photos and pick the most balanced one.

**Shots:**
- Direct sun — facing it
- Full shade
- Partial shade
- Angled to the left of the sun
- Angled to the right of the sun

## Lesson 9 — Indoor Low Light
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Low Light Problem [Teach]
Photos get grainy and soft indoors.

### Slide 2 — Move to Light Sources [Teach]
Position yourself near windows, lamps, or screens to improve the light.

### Slide 3 — Stability Matters More [Teach]
In low light, movement blurs everything. Hold your phone steady or rest it on a surface.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take 5 indoor photos using different light sources.

**Shots:**
- Window light
- Lamp light
- Screen light
- Two sources combined
- Steadied shot in low light

## Lesson 10 — Movement & Story
3 slides · 1 challenge

### Slide 1 — Static Photos Are Limited [Teach]
Movement adds life.

### Slide 2 — Capture Action [Teach]
Look for natural movement: walking, turning, laughing, interacting.

### Slide 3 — Sequence Thinking [Teach]
Take multiple shots in a sequence, not singles. The best frame is rarely the first.

### Slide 4 — Your Challenge [Challenge]
Take a 5-photo sequence of one action and pick the strongest frame.

**Shots:**
- Before the action starts
- Action beginning
- Peak of the action
- Action ending
- After the action
